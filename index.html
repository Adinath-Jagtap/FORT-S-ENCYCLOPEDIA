About Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

The Legendary Maratha Warrior King

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630-1680) was the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India. He is considered one of the greatest warriors in Indian history, known for his military genius, progressive administration, and the numerous forts he built or captured throughout Maharashtra.

Under his leadership, the Marathas became a formidable force against the Mughal Empire. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's innovative guerrilla warfare tactics and strategic fort development created a network of strongholds that protected the Maratha territory.

His military strategies involved a careful study of terrain, swift cavalry movements, and the development of hill forts as defensive structures. These forts were not merely military installations but self-sufficient ecosystems with temples, granaries, water storage systems, and living quarters.

Featured Forts

Discover some of the most impressive and historically significant fortresses of the reign of Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Raigad Fort

Mahad, Raigad

Capital fort of the Maratha Empire, where Shivaji Maharaj was crowned and later laid to rest. This mountain fortress served as the central seat of power.

Sindhudurg Fort

Malvan, Konkan

A magnificent sea fort built on an island in the Arabian Sea, showcasing Shivaji's naval prowess and maritime vision for the Maratha Empire.

Pratapgad Fort

Satara District

Site of the famous battle where Shivaji Maharaj defeated Afzal Khan, a turning point in the establishment of the Maratha Empire.

Rajgad Fort

Pune District

One of Shivaji's favorite forts, serving as his capital for over 26 years before Raigad. Features impressive fortifications and water systems.

Sinhagad Fort

Pune

Known for the Battle of Sinhagad, where Tanaji Malusare sacrificed his life to recapture this strategic fort for Shivaji Maharaj.

Vijaydurg Fort

Sindhudurg District

The oldest and one of the strongest marine forts on India's west coast, known as the 'Victory Fort' and a key base for Maratha naval operations.
